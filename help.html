    • Frequently Asked Questions

    How does OneCommunity work?

    OneCommunity connects businesses, charities, and volunteers to facilitate donations, volunteering opportunities, and community support. We provide a platform for in-kind donations, volunteer matching, and community collaboration.

    What types of donations do you accept?

    We facilitate both in-kind donations (goods, services, equipment) and monetary donations. In-kind donations are processed directly between businesses and charities. For monetary donations, we charge a 4.5% commission to cover platform costs and ensure sustainable operation.

    How much is the commission on monetary donations?

    We charge a 4.5% commission on all monetary donations processed through our platform. This fee helps cover our operational costs, platform maintenance, and ensures we can continue providing our services to the community.

    Why do you charge a commission?

    The 4.5% commission allows us to maintain and improve our platform, provide customer support, and ensure secure payment processing. This fee is transparent and clearly communicated before any donation is processed.

    Are there fees for in-kind donations?

    No, there are no fees for in-kind donations (goods, services, equipment). These are processed directly between businesses and charities without any commission.

    How do I know my donation is going to the right place?

    All charities on our platform are validated and verified. We work with registered charities and conduct due diligence to ensure legitimacy. You can view charity details and verification status on our platform.

    • Campaign & lobby

    Experts have warned that, "To have these horrifying levels of destitution in a country like ours is a political choice." Our government must take urgent action. Campaigning and advocacy are about persuasion, influencing for change, and demonstrating value. You can advocate to anybody: friends, family, the media, bloggers, sponsors and funders.

    Writing to politicians is a great way to raise awareness of child poverty because it's a quick, easy and effective way to make your voice heard. Politicians have the power to reduce child poverty on a national scale; by raising these issues with your MP, you're adding your voice to a national campaign demanding change. It's your MP's job to make your voice heard in Parliament, even if you did not vote for them. They represent their local area and can raise the concerns of local people by voting on issues in Parliament; writing to the government minister responsible for the issue or making an appointment to see them; asking Parliamentary Questions about issues you raise or asking for a debate in Parliament - a relevant minister will always attend this debate to respond to your MP on behalf of the government. MPs are unlikely to pay any attention if they think an issue doesn't affect their area, which is why it is so important for you to share your story.
